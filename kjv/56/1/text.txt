Paul, a servant of God and an apostle of Jesus Christ, is called according to the faith of God's chosen ones and the acknowledgment of the truth that leads to godliness.
In hope of eternal life, which God, who cannot lie, promised before the world began.
But in due time, He has revealed His word through preaching, which has been entrusted to me according to the commandment of God our Savior.
To Titus, my true son in our common faith: Grace, mercy, and peace from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ our Savior.
For this reason, I left you in Crete, so that you might set in order what remains and appoint elders in every city, as I directed you.
If anyone is blameless, the husband of one wife, with faithful children who are not accused of being wild or disobedient.
For a bishop must be blameless, as a steward of God; not self-willed, not quick-tempered, not given to wine, not violent, not pursuing dishonest gain;
But rather a lover of hospitality, a lover of good, sober-minded, just, holy, and self-controlled;
Holding firmly to the faithful word as he has been taught, so that he may be able to encourage others by sound doctrine and refute those who oppose it.
For there are many rebellious people, full of empty talk and deception, especially those of the circumcision group.
Their mouths must be stopped; they are upsetting whole families by teaching things they ought not to teach for the sake of dishonest gain.
One of their own prophets said, "Cretans are always liars, evil beasts, lazy gluttons."
This testimony is true. Therefore, rebuke them sharply, so that they may be sound in the faith;
Not paying attention to Jewish myths and the commands of men who turn away from the truth.
To the pure, all things are pure; but to those who are defiled and unbelieving, nothing is pure; their minds and consciences are also defiled.
They claim to know God, but by their actions, they deny Him; they are detestable, disobedient, and unfit for any good work.
