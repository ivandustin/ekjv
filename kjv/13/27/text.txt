The children of Israel were counted, including the chief fathers, captains of thousands and hundreds, and their officers who served the king in various capacities. Each month, throughout the entire year, there were twenty-four thousand in every course.
In the first month, Jashobeam the son of Zabdiel was over the first course, which also consisted of twenty-four thousand.
The chief of all the captains for the first month was from the children of Perez.
In the second month, Dodai the Ahohite was in charge, with Mikloth as the ruler of his course, which also had twenty-four thousand.
Benaiah the son of Jehoiada, a chief priest, was the third captain for the third month, and his course also included twenty-four thousand.
This Benaiah was renowned among the thirty and even above them, and his son Ammizabad was part of his course.
Asahel, the brother of Joab, was the fourth captain for the fourth month, followed by his son Zebadiah, and their course also had twenty-four thousand.
Shamhuth the Izrahite was the fifth captain for the fifth month, and his course also consisted of twenty-four thousand.
Ira the son of Ikkesh the Tekoite was the sixth captain for the sixth month, and his course also included twenty-four thousand.
Helez the Pelonite, from the children of Ephraim, was the seventh captain for the seventh month, and his course also had twenty-four thousand.
Sibbecai the Hushathite, from the Zarhites, was the eighth captain for the eighth month, and his course also consisted of twenty-four thousand.
Abiezer the Anetothite, from the Benjamites, was the ninth captain for the ninth month, and his course also included twenty-four thousand.
Maharai the Netophathite, from the Zarhites, was the tenth captain for the tenth month, and his course also had twenty-four thousand.
Benaiah the Pirathonite, from the children of Ephraim, was the eleventh captain for the eleventh month, and his course also consisted of twenty-four thousand.
Heldai the Netophathite, from Othniel, was the twelfth captain for the twelfth month, and his course also included twenty-four thousand.
Additionally, the rulers over the tribes of Israel were as follows: Eliezer the son of Zichri for the Reubenites, and Shephatiah the son of Maachah for the Simeonites.
For the Levites, Hashabiah the son of Kemuel was in charge, and for the Aaronites, it was Zadok.
Elihu, a brother of David, was the ruler for Judah, while Omri the son of Michael represented Issachar.
Ishmaiah the son of Obadiah was the ruler for Zebulun, and Jerimoth the son of Azriel for Naphtali.
Hoshea the son of Azaziah was in charge of the children of Ephraim, and Joel the son of Pedaiah represented the half tribe of Manasseh.
Iddo the son of Zechariah was the ruler for the half tribe of Manasseh in Gilead, and Jaasiel the son of Abner for Benjamin.
Azareel the son of Jeroham was the ruler for Dan. These were the princes of the tribes of Israel.
However, David did not count those twenty years old and under, as the LORD had declared He would increase Israel like the stars in the heavens.
Joab the son of Zeruiah began the numbering but did not complete it, as there was wrath against Israel for this; thus, the number was not included in the chronicles of King David.
Azmaveth the son of Adiel was in charge of the king's treasures, while Jehonathan the son of Uzziah oversaw the storehouses in the fields, cities, villages, and castles.
Ezri the son of Chelub was responsible for those working the fields for tillage.
Shimei the Ramathite was in charge of the vineyards, and Zabdi the Shiphmite managed the increase of the vineyards for the wine cellars.
Baalhanan the Gederite oversaw the olive and sycamore trees in the low plains, while Joash was in charge of the oil cellars.
Shitrai the Sharonite managed the herds feeding in Sharon, and Shaphat the son of Adlai oversaw the herds in the valleys.
Obil the Ishmaelite was responsible for the camels, and Jehdeiah the Meronothite managed the donkeys.
Jaziz the Hagerite was in charge of the flocks. All these were the rulers of the resources belonging to King David.
Jonathan, David's uncle, served as a counselor, a wise man, and a scribe, alongside Jehiel the son of Hachmoni, who was with the king's sons.
Ahithophel was the king's counselor, and Hushai the Archite was the king's companion.
Following Ahithophel were Jehoiada the son of Benaiah and Abiathar, while Joab was the general of the king's army.
