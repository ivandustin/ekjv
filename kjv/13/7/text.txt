The sons of Issachar were Tola, Puah, Jashub, and Shimrom, totaling four.  
The sons of Tola were Uzzi, Rephaiah, Jeriel, Jahmai, Jibsam, and Shemuel, heads of their father's house, specifically Tola. They were valiant men of great strength in their generations, with a count of twenty-two thousand six hundred during the days of David.  
The sons of Uzzi were Izrahiah, and the sons of Izrahiah included Michael, Obadiah, Joel, and Ishiah, totaling five, all of whom were chief men.  
Along with them, by their generations and according to their father's house, there were bands of soldiers for war, numbering thirty-six thousand men, as they had many wives and sons.  
Their brethren among all the families of Issachar were valiant men of strength, totaling eighty-seven thousand when counted by their genealogies.  
The sons of Benjamin were Bela, Becher, and Jediael, totaling three.  
The sons of Bela were Ezbon, Uzzi, Uzziel, Jerimoth, and Iri, totaling five; they were heads of their father's house and mighty men of valor, counted by their genealogies as twenty-two thousand thirty-four.  
The sons of Becher were Zemira, Joash, Eliezer, Elioenai, Omri, Jerimoth, Abiah, Anathoth, and Alameth. These are the sons of Becher.  
The total number of them, according to their genealogy and by their generations, heads of their father's house and mighty men of valor, was twenty thousand two hundred.  
The sons of Jediael were Bilhan, and the sons of Bilhan included Jeush, Benjamin, Ehud, Chenaanah, Zethan, Tharshish, and Ahishahar.  
All these were the sons of Jediael, heads of their fathers' houses, mighty men of valor, totaling seventeen thousand two hundred soldiers fit for war and battle.  
Shuppim and Huppim were the children of Ir, and Hushim were the sons of Aher.  
The sons of Naphtali were Jahziel, Guni, Jezer, and Shallum, the sons of Bilhah.  
The sons of Manasseh included Ashriel, whom she bore; (his concubine, the Aramitess, bore Machir, the father of Gilead).  
Machir took to wife the sister of Huppim and Shuppim, whose sister's name was Maachah; the name of the second was Zelophehad, who had daughters.  
Maachah, the wife of Machir, bore a son and named him Peresh; his brother was named Sheresh, and his sons were Ulam and Rakem.  
The sons of Ulam were Bedan. These were the sons of Gilead, the son of Machir, the son of Manasseh.  
His sister Hammoleketh bore Ishod, Abiezer, and Mahalah.  
The sons of Shemidah were Ahian, Shechem, Likhi, and Aniam.  
The sons of Ephraim were Shuthelah, Bered his son, Tahath his son, Eladah his son, and Tahath his son.  
Zabad was his son, Shuthelah his son, Ezer, and Elead, whom the men of Gath, born in that land, killed because they came down to take their cattle.  
Ephraim, their father, mourned for many days, and his brethren came to comfort him.  
When he went in to his wife, she conceived and bore a son, whom he named Beriah, because it went badly for his house.  
(And his daughter was Sherah, who built the lower and upper Bethhoron, and Uzzensherah.)  
Rephah was his son, as well as Resheph, Telah his son, and Tahan his son.  
Laadan was his son, Ammihud his son, and Elishama his son.  
Non was his son, and Jehoshuah was his son.  
Their possessions and habitations included Bethel and its towns, eastward Naaran, and westward Gezer with its towns; Shechem and its towns, extending to Gaza and its towns.  
By the borders of the children of Manasseh were Bethshean and its towns, Taanach and its towns, Megiddo and its towns, and Dor and its towns. In these dwelt the children of Joseph, the son of Israel.  
The sons of Asher were Imnah, Isuah, Ishuai, Beriah, and their sister Serah.  
The sons of Beriah were Heber and Malchiel, who is the father of Birzavith.  
Heber begat Japhlet, Shomer, Hotham, and their sister Shua.  
The sons of Japhlet were Pasach, Bimhal, and Ashvath. These are the children of Japhlet.  
The sons of Shamer were Ahi, Rohgah, Jehubbah, and Aram.  
The sons of his brother Helem were Zophah, Imna, Shelesh, and Amal.  
The sons of Zophah were Suah, Harnepher, Shual, Beri, and Imrah,  
Bezer, Hod, Shamma, Shilshah, Ithran, and Beera.  
The sons of Jether were Jephunneh, Pispah, and Ara.  
The sons of Ulla were Arah, Haniel, and Rezia.  
All these were the children of Asher, heads of their father's house, choice and mighty men of valor, chief among the princes. The total number throughout their genealogy of those fit for war and battle was twenty-six thousand men.
