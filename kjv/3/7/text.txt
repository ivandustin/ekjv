1. This is the law regarding the trespass offering: it is considered most holy.
2. The trespass offering shall be killed in the same place as the burnt offering, and its blood shall be sprinkled around the altar.
3. The entire fat portion must be offered, including the rump and the fat covering the innards.
4. Additionally, the two kidneys and the fat on them, located by the flanks, along with the caul above the liver, must be removed.
5. The priest shall burn these on the altar as a fire offering to the LORD: this is a trespass offering.
6. Every male among the priests may eat of it; it must be consumed in the holy place, as it is most holy.
7. The law for the sin offering is the same as for the trespass offering: there is one law for both, and the priest who makes atonement with it shall have it.
8. The priest who offers any man's burnt offering shall keep the skin of the burnt offering he has presented.
9. All meat offerings baked in the oven, or prepared in a frying pan or on a griddle, shall belong to the priest who offers them.
10. Every meat offering mixed with oil or dry shall be shared equally among all the sons of Aaron.
11. This is the law for the sacrifice of peace offerings that he shall present to the LORD.
12. If he offers it as a thanksgiving sacrifice, he must include unleavened cakes mixed with oil, unleavened wafers anointed with oil, and fine flour cakes fried in oil.
13. In addition to the cakes, he shall offer leavened bread with the thanksgiving sacrifice of his peace offerings.
14. From the entire offering, he shall present one portion as a heave offering to the LORD, which shall belong to the priest who sprinkles the blood of the peace offerings.
15. The flesh of the thanksgiving peace offering must be eaten on the same day it is offered; none of it should be left until morning.
16. If the sacrifice is a vow or a voluntary offering, it may also be eaten on the same day it is offered, and any remainder can be consumed the next day.
17. However, any leftover flesh from the sacrifice on the third day must be burned with fire.
18. If any flesh from the peace offering is eaten on the third day, it will not be accepted, and the person who eats it will bear the consequences: it will be considered an abomination.
19. Flesh that touches anything unclean must not be eaten; it should be burned with fire. Clean flesh may be eaten.
20. Anyone who eats the flesh of the peace offering that belongs to the LORD while in a state of uncleanness shall be cut off from their people.
21. Furthermore, anyone who touches any unclean thing, whether it be human uncleanness, an unclean animal, or any abominable thing, and then eats the flesh of the peace offering that belongs to the LORD, shall also be cut off from their people.
22. The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,
23. Speak to the children of Israel, telling them that they must not eat any fat from oxen, sheep, or goats.
24. The fat from animals that die of themselves or are torn by beasts may be used for other purposes, but you must not eat it.
25. Anyone who eats the fat of an animal offered as a fire sacrifice to the LORD shall be cut off from their people.
26. You must not consume any blood, whether from birds or animals, in any of your dwellings.
27. Anyone who eats any blood shall be cut off from their people.
28. The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,
29. Speak to the children of Israel, instructing them that anyone who offers a peace offering to the LORD must bring their offering to the LORD.
30. Their own hands shall present the offerings made by fire to the LORD, including the fat with the breast, which shall be waved as a wave offering before the LORD.
31. The priest shall burn the fat on the altar, but the breast shall belong to Aaron and his sons.
32. You shall give the right shoulder to the priest as a heave offering from your peace offerings.
33. The son of Aaron who offers the blood and fat of the peace offerings shall receive the right shoulder as his portion.
34. I have taken the wave breast and the heave shoulder from the children of Israel's peace offerings and given them to Aaron the priest and his sons as a perpetual statute among the children of Israel.
35. This is the portion designated for the anointing of Aaron and his sons from the offerings made by fire to the LORD, on the day they were appointed to serve as priests.
36. The LORD commanded that these be given to them by the children of Israel on the day they were anointed, as a lasting ordinance for all generations.
37. This is the law concerning the burnt offering, the meat offering, the sin offering, the trespass offering, the consecrations, and the peace offerings.
38. The LORD commanded Moses on Mount Sinai, on the day He instructed the children of Israel to present their offerings to the LORD in the wilderness of Sinai.
