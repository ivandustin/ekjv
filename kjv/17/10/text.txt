And King Ahasuerus imposed a tribute on the land and the islands of the sea.
All the deeds of his power and might, along with the proclamation of Mordecai's greatness, to which the king elevated him, are they not recorded in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Media and Persia?
For Mordecai the Jew was second in command to King Ahasuerus, esteemed among the Jews, and well-regarded by the majority of his brethren, striving for the prosperity of his people and promoting peace for all his descendants.
