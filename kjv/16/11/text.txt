The rulers of the people resided in Jerusalem, while the remaining populace cast lots to determine that one out of ten would live in Jerusalem, the holy city, and the other nine would inhabit various other cities.
The people expressed their gratitude to all those who willingly chose to reside in Jerusalem.
These are the leaders of the province who lived in Jerusalem; however, in the cities of Judah, each person dwelled in their own property, including the Israelites, priests, Levites, Nethinims, and the descendants of Solomon's servants.
In Jerusalem, certain descendants of Judah and Benjamin resided. Among the children of Judah were Athaiah, the son of Uzziah, who was the son of Zechariah, the son of Amariah, the son of Shephatiah, the son of Mahalaleel, from the lineage of Perez.
Maaseiah, the son of Baruch, was also listed, being the son of Colhozeh, the son of Hazaiah, the son of Adaiah, the son of Joiarib, the son of Zechariah, the son of Shiloni.
All the sons of Perez who lived in Jerusalem numbered four hundred sixty-eight valiant men.
The sons of Benjamin included Sallu, the son of Meshullam, who was the son of Joed, the son of Pedaiah, the son of Kolaiah, the son of Maaseiah, the son of Ithiel, the son of Jesaiah.
Following him were Gabbai and Sallai, totaling nine hundred twenty-eight.
Joel, the son of Zichri, served as their overseer, while Judah, the son of Senuah, was second in command over the city.
Among the priests were Jedaiah, the son of Joiarib, and Jachin.
Seraiah, the son of Hilkiah, who was the son of Meshullam, the son of Zadok, the son of Meraioth, the son of Ahitub, served as the ruler of the house of God.
Their brethren who worked in the house numbered eight hundred twenty-two, including Adaiah, the son of Jeroham, the son of Pelaliah, the son of Amzi, the son of Zechariah, the son of Pashur, the son of Malchiah.
His brethren, the heads of families, numbered two hundred forty-two, including Amashai, the son of Azareel, the son of Ahasai, the son of Meshillemoth, the son of Immer.
Their brethren, who were mighty men of valor, numbered one hundred twenty-eight, with Zabdiel, the son of one of the prominent men, as their overseer.
Among the Levites were Shemaiah, the son of Hashub, the son of Azrikam, the son of Hashabiah, the son of Bunni.
Shabbethai and Jozabad, leaders among the Levites, oversaw the external affairs of the house of God.
Mattaniah, the son of Micha, the son of Zabdi, the son of Asaph, was the principal who initiated the thanksgiving in prayer; Bakbukiah was second among his brethren, and Abda, the son of Shammua, the son of Galal, the son of Jeduthun, was also noted.
The total number of Levites in the holy city was two hundred eighty-four.
Additionally, the porters, Akkub, Talmon, and their brethren who guarded the gates, numbered one hundred seventy-two.
The remaining Israelites, including priests and Levites, lived in all the cities of Judah, each in their own inheritance.
The Nethinims resided in Ophel, with Ziha and Gispa overseeing them.
The overseer of the Levites in Jerusalem was Uzzi, the son of Bani, the son of Hashabiah, the son of Mattaniah, the son of Micha. The sons of Asaph were responsible for the music in the house of God.
The king had commanded that a specific portion be allocated for the singers, to be provided daily.
Pethahiah, the son of Meshezabeel, from the children of Zerah, the son of Judah, was at the king's side regarding all matters concerning the people.
In the villages and their fields, some of the children of Judah lived in Kirjatharba and its surrounding villages, in Dibon and its villages, and in Jekabzeel and its villages.
They also resided in Jeshua, Moladah, and Bethphelet,
as well as in Hazarshual, Beersheba, and its villages,
in Ziklag, Mekonah, and its villages,
and in Enrimmon, Zareah, and Jarmuth,
Zanoah, Adullam, and their villages, Lachish and its fields, Azekah and its villages. They lived from Beersheba to the valley of Hinnom.
The children of Benjamin from Geba lived in Michmash, Aija, Bethel, and their villages.
They also resided in Anathoth, Nob, Ananiah,
Hazor, Ramah, Gittaim,
Hadid, Zeboim, Neballat,
Lod, and Ono, in the valley of craftsmen.
The Levites were organized into divisions in both Judah and Benjamin.
