Here is the edited version of each line:
1. This is what you shall do to consecrate them for their ministry in the priest's office: Take one young bull and two rams without blemish.
2. Also, take unleavened bread, cakes of unleavened bread mixed with oil, and wafers of unleavened bread anointed with oil; you shall make them from fine flour.
3. Place them in one basket and bring the basket along with the bull and the two rams.
4. Bring Aaron and his sons to the entrance of the tabernacle of the congregation and wash them with water.
5. Take the garments and put on Aaron the tunic, the robe of the ephod, the ephod, and the breastplate, and fasten the intricately woven sash of the ephod around him.
6. Place the turban on his head and set the holy crown on the turban.
7. Then take the anointing oil, pour it on his head, and anoint him.
8. Bring his sons and put tunics on them.
9. Fasten sashes around Aaron and his sons, and put bonnets on them; the priesthood shall be theirs as a lasting ordinance, and you shall consecrate Aaron and his sons.
10. Bring a bull before the tabernacle of the congregation, and Aaron and his sons shall lay their hands on the head of the bull.
11. You shall kill the bull before the LORD at the entrance of the tabernacle of the congregation.
12. Take some of the bull's blood and put it on the horns of the altar with your finger, and pour out the rest of the blood at the base of the altar.
13. Take all the fat that covers the entrails, the lobe of the liver, the two kidneys, and the fat on them, and burn them on the altar.
14. But the flesh of the bull, along with its skin and dung, you shall burn with fire outside the camp; it is a sin offering.
15. You shall also take one ram, and Aaron and his sons shall lay their hands on the head of the ram.
16. You shall kill the ram, take its blood, and sprinkle it around the altar.
17. Cut the ram into pieces, wash its entrails and legs, and place them with the pieces and the head.
18. Burn the entire ram on the altar; it is a burnt offering to the LORD, a pleasing aroma, an offering made by fire to the LORD.
19. Take the other ram, and Aaron and his sons shall lay their hands on the head of the ram.
20. Kill the ram, take some of its blood, and put it on the tip of Aaron's right ear, the tip of his sons' right ears, the thumb of their right hands, and the big toe of their right feet, and sprinkle the blood around the altar.
21. Take some of the blood from the altar and some of the anointing oil, and sprinkle it on Aaron, his garments, his sons, and their garments with him; he and his garments shall be consecrated, as well as his sons and their garments.
22. Also, take the fat of the ram, the fat tail, the fat covering the entrails, the lobe of the liver, the two kidneys, the fat on them, and the right shoulder; for it is a ram of consecration.
23. Include one loaf of bread, one cake of oiled bread, and one wafer from the basket of unleavened bread that is before the LORD.
24. Place all these in the hands of Aaron and his sons, and wave them as a wave offering before the LORD.
25. Receive them from their hands and burn them on the altar as a burnt offering, a pleasing aroma before the LORD; it is an offering made by fire to the LORD.
26. Take the breast of the ram of Aaron's consecration and wave it as a wave offering before the LORD; it shall be your portion.
27. Sanctify the breast of the wave offering and the shoulder of the heave offering, which is waved and heaved up, from the ram of consecration, for Aaron and his sons.
28. This shall be theirs as a lasting ordinance from the children of Israel; it is a heave offering from the sacrifices of their peace offerings, a heave offering to the LORD.
29. The holy garments of Aaron shall be passed down to his sons after him, to be anointed and consecrated in them.
30. The son who becomes priest in his place shall wear them for seven days when he enters the tabernacle of the congregation to minister in the holy place.
31. Take the ram of consecration and boil its flesh in the holy place.
32. Aaron and his sons shall eat the flesh of the ram and the bread that is in the basket at the entrance of the tabernacle of the congregation.
33. They shall eat those things by which atonement was made to consecrate and sanctify them; but a stranger shall not eat of them, for they are holy.
34. If any of the flesh of the consecrations or of the bread remains until morning, you shall burn the remainder with fire; it shall not be eaten, for it is holy.
35. You shall do all these things to Aaron and his sons according to all that I have commanded you; you shall consecrate them for seven days.
36. Each day you shall offer a bull for a sin offering for atonement; cleanse the altar when you have made atonement for it, and anoint it to sanctify it.
37. For seven days you shall make atonement for the altar and sanctify it; the altar shall be most holy; whatever touches the altar shall be holy.
38. Now this is what you shall offer on the altar: two lambs of the first year, day by day continually.
39. Offer one lamb in the morning and the other lamb at twilight.
40. With the one lamb, offer a tenth of an ephah of flour mixed with a fourth of a hin of beaten oil, and a fourth of a hin of wine for a drink offering.
41. The other lamb you shall offer at twilight, and you shall do with it according to the grain offering of the morning and according to its drink offering, for a pleasing aroma, an offering made by fire to the LORD.
42. This shall be a continual burnt offering throughout your generations at the entrance of the tabernacle of the congregation before the LORD, where I will meet you to speak with you.
43. There I will meet with the children of Israel, and the tabernacle shall be sanctified by my glory.
44. I will sanctify the tabernacle of the congregation and the altar; I will also sanctify both Aaron and his sons to minister to me in the priest's office.
45. I will dwell among the children of Israel and be their God.
46. They shall know that I am the LORD their God, who brought them out of the land of Egypt, that I may dwell among them; I am the LORD their God.
