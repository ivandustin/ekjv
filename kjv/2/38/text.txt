He constructed the altar of burnt offering using shittim wood, measuring five cubits in length and five cubits in breadth; it was square and three cubits high.
He fashioned horns for the altar at its four corners, made from the same material, and overlaid it with brass.
He created all the vessels for the altar, including pots, shovels, basins, fleshhooks, and firepans, all made of brass.
He designed a brass grate of network for the altar, positioned beneath it, extending to its middle.
He cast four rings for the four corners of the brass grate, serving as places for the staves.
He made the staves from shittim wood and overlaid them with brass.
He inserted the staves into the rings on the sides of the altar for transport; the altar was constructed hollow with boards.
He crafted a laver of brass, along with its foot, made from the looking glasses of the women who assembled at the entrance of the tabernacle of the congregation.
He built the court: on the south side, the hangings were made of fine twined linen, measuring one hundred cubits.
There were twenty pillars with twenty brass sockets; the hooks of the pillars and their fillets were made of silver.
For the north side, the hangings also measured one hundred cubits, with twenty pillars and twenty brass sockets; the hooks and fillets were silver.
The west side featured hangings of fifty cubits, supported by ten pillars and ten sockets; the hooks and fillets were silver.
The east side measured fifty cubits.
The hangings on one side of the gate were fifteen cubits, with three pillars and three sockets.
On the opposite side of the court gate, there were also hangings of fifteen cubits, with three pillars and three sockets.
All the hangings surrounding the court were made of fine twined linen.
The sockets for the pillars were brass; the hooks and fillets of the pillars were silver, with silver overlay on their chapiters, and all the pillars of the court were filleted with silver.
The hanging for the gate of the court was intricately designed, made of blue, purple, scarlet, and fine twined linen, measuring twenty cubits in length and five cubits in height, matching the hangings of the court.
There were four pillars with four brass sockets; their hooks were silver, and their chapiters and fillets were also silver.
All the pins for the tabernacle and the surrounding court were made of brass.
This is the total for the tabernacle, known as the tabernacle of testimony, as it was accounted for according to Moses' command, for the service of the Levites, by the hand of Ithamar, son of Aaron the priest.
Bezaleel, son of Uri, son of Hur, from the tribe of Judah, made everything the LORD commanded Moses.
Alongside him was Aholiab, son of Ahisamach, from the tribe of Dan, an engraver, skilled craftsman, and embroiderer in blue, purple, scarlet, and fine linen.
The total gold used for the work in the holy place, including the gold from the offerings, amounted to twenty-nine talents and seven hundred thirty shekels, according to the sanctuary's shekel.
The silver from those counted in the congregation totaled one hundred talents and one thousand seven hundred sixty-five shekels, according to the sanctuary's shekel.
Each man contributed a bekah, which is half a shekel, according to the sanctuary's shekel, for every individual counted, from twenty years old and upward, totaling six hundred three thousand five hundred fifty men.
From the one hundred talents of silver, they cast the sockets for the sanctuary and the sockets for the veil; one hundred sockets were made, one talent for each socket.
From the one thousand seven hundred seventy-five shekels, he made hooks for the pillars, overlaid their chapiters, and filleted them.
The brass from the offerings amounted to seventy talents and two thousand four hundred shekels.
With this brass, he made the sockets for the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, the brass altar, the brass grate for it, and all the vessels of the altar.
He also made the sockets for the court surrounding it, the sockets for the court gate, and all the pins for the tabernacle and the court.
