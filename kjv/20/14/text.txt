Every wise woman builds her house, but the foolish tears it down with her own hands.
He who walks in his integrity fears the LORD, but he who is perverse in his ways despises Him.
In the mouth of the foolish is a rod of pride, but the lips of the wise preserve them.
Where there are no oxen, the manger is clean, but much increase comes from the strength of the ox.
A faithful witness does not lie, but a false witness speaks lies.
A scoffer seeks wisdom and finds it not, but knowledge is easy for him who understands.
Avoid the presence of a foolish man when you do not perceive in him the lips of knowledge.
The wisdom of the prudent is to understand his way, but the folly of fools is deceit.
Fools mock at sin, but among the righteous there is favor.
The heart knows its own bitterness, and a stranger does not intermeddle with its joy.
The house of the wicked will be overthrown, but the dwelling of the upright will flourish.
There is a way that seems right to a man, but its end is the way of death.
Even in laughter, the heart is sorrowful, and the end of that mirth is heaviness.
The backslider in heart will be filled with his own ways, but a good man will be satisfied with himself.
The simple believe every word, but the prudent man carefully considers his steps.
A wise man fears and departs from evil, but the fool rages and is confident.
He who is quick to anger acts foolishly, and a man of wicked schemes is hated.
The simple inherit folly, but the prudent are crowned with knowledge.
The evil bow before the good, and the wicked stand at the gates of the righteous.
The poor is despised even by his own neighbor, but the rich has many friends.
He who despises his neighbor sins, but he who shows mercy to the poor is happy.
Do they not err who devise evil? But mercy and truth will be to those who devise good.
In all labor there is profit, but mere talk leads only to poverty.
The crown of the wise is their riches, but the foolishness of fools is folly.
A true witness saves lives, but a deceitful witness speaks lies.
In the fear of the LORD is strong confidence, and His children will have a place of refuge.
The fear of the LORD is a fountain of life, turning one away from the snares of death.
In the multitude of people is the king's honor, but in the lack of people is the destruction of the prince.
He who is slow to anger has great understanding, but he who is quick-tempered exalts folly.
A sound heart is the life of the flesh, but envy rots the bones.
He who oppresses the poor insults his Maker, but he who honors Him has mercy on the poor.
The wicked are driven away in their wickedness, but the righteous have hope in their death.
Wisdom rests in the heart of him who has understanding, but what is in the midst of fools is made known.
Righteousness exalts a nation, but sin is a reproach to any people.
The king's favor is toward a wise servant, but his wrath is against him who causes shame.
