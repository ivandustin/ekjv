Better is a dry morsel accompanied by peace than a house full of sacrifices accompanied by strife.
A wise servant will rule over a son who brings shame and will share in the inheritance among the brothers.
The refining pot is for silver, and the furnace is for gold, but the LORD tests the hearts.
A wicked person pays attention to false lips, and a liar listens to a deceitful tongue.
Whoever mocks the poor insults their Maker, and those who rejoice in calamities will not go unpunished.
Grandchildren are the crown of the elderly, and the glory of children is their fathers.
Excellent speech is not fitting for a fool; much less are lying lips suitable for a prince.
A gift is like a precious stone in the eyes of the one who possesses it; wherever it goes, it brings prosperity.
He who covers a transgression seeks love, but he who repeats a matter separates close friends.
A reproof penetrates deeper into a wise man than a hundred stripes into a fool.
An evil person seeks only rebellion; therefore, a cruel messenger will be sent against him.
It is better for a man to encounter a bear robbed of her cubs than to meet a fool in his folly.
Whoever rewards evil for good will have evil never depart from his house.
The beginning of strife is like releasing water; therefore, stop contention before it escalates.
He who justifies the wicked and condemns the just are both an abomination to the LORD.
Why is there a price in the hand of a fool to acquire wisdom, seeing he has no heart for it?
A friend loves at all times, and a brother is born for times of adversity.
A man lacking understanding strikes hands and becomes surety in the presence of his friend.
He who loves transgression loves strife, and he who exalts his gate seeks destruction.
He who has a perverse heart finds no good, and he who has a deceitful tongue falls into mischief.
He who begets a fool does so to his sorrow, and the father of a fool has no joy.
A merry heart is like medicine, but a broken spirit dries the bones.
A wicked man takes a gift from his bosom to pervert the ways of justice.
Wisdom is before the one who has understanding, but the eyes of a fool wander to the ends of the earth.
A foolish son brings grief to his father and bitterness to his mother.
It is not good to punish the just, nor is it right to strike princes for their integrity.
He who has knowledge spares his words, and a man of understanding has an excellent spirit.
Even a fool, when he remains silent, is considered wise, and he who shuts his lips is regarded as a man of understanding.
