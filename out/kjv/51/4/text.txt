Masters, grant your servants what is just and fair, knowing that you too have a Master in heaven.
Continue in prayer, staying vigilant with gratitude;
And pray for us as well, that God may open a door for us to speak the mystery of Christ, for which I am also imprisoned:
So that I may clearly proclaim it, as I should.
Walk wisely toward those outside, making the most of every opportunity.
Let your conversation always be gracious, seasoned with salt, so that you may know how to respond to each person.
Tychicus will inform you of my circumstances; he is a beloved brother, a faithful minister, and a fellow servant in the Lord:
I have sent him to you for this very purpose, to learn about your situation and to encourage your hearts;
Along with Onesimus, a faithful and beloved brother, who is one of you. They will inform you of everything happening here.
Aristarchus, my fellow prisoner, sends his greetings, as does Marcus, the cousin of Barnabas (about whom you received instructions: if he comes to you, welcome him);
And Jesus, who is called Justus, who are of the circumcision. These are my only fellow workers for the kingdom of God, who have been a source of comfort to me.
Epaphras, who is one of you, a servant of Christ, sends his greetings, always striving earnestly for you in prayers, that you may stand mature and fully assured in all the will of God.
For I can testify that he has great zeal for you, as well as for those in Laodicea and Hierapolis.
Luke, the beloved physician, and Demas send their greetings.
Greet the brothers and sisters in Laodicea, and Nymphas, and the church that meets in his house.
And when this letter is read among you, see that it is also read in the church of the Laodiceans; and that you in turn read the letter from Laodicea.
And tell Archippus, "See to it that you complete the ministry you have received in the Lord."
This greeting is in my own hand, Paul. Remember my chains. Grace be with you. Amen.
